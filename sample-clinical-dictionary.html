Clinical terms with useful comparisons.

Inside HealthPath, each field has a searchable dictionary. This preview shows the style: definition, why it matters, and what learners commonly confuse it with.

Infection control

Contact precautions

Meaning: Extra gown/glove and equipment workflow for organisms spread by touch.

Often confused with: Droplet precautions, which focus on respiratory droplets.

Labs

Sensitivity vs specificity

Sensitivity: Good at catching disease when it is present. Specificity: Good at confirming disease when positive.

Why it matters: Screening and confirmatory tests answer different questions.

Public health

Baseline risk

Meaning: The starting chance of an outcome before an exposure, treatment, or intervention.

Often missed: Relative risk sounds dramatic without absolute risk context.

Medication safety

Therapeutic index

Meaning: How close an effective dose is to a harmful dose.

Why it matters: Narrow therapeutic index drugs need more careful monitoring.
